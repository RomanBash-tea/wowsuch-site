When the 18 months are up

Your chips are paid off. There's no balloon payment and no surprise — you simply pick one of three paths.

A

Own the whole L9

Pay the difference between what you've already paid and the $6,000 whole-L9 basis, and the entire 288-chip machine is yours — hosted, hashing, and merge-mining both coins. Keep earning daily LTC + DOGE.

B

Walk away

Done mining? Simply walk away at the end of the term. Nothing further is owed, and you keep every coin already paid out to your own MPC wallet along the way.

C

Chip as a keepsake

Have your actual chip desoldered from the hashboard and shipped to you as a souvenir of your first mine. Note: a single chip cannot mine on its own, so a shipped chip is a keepsake, not a working miner.

Repairs included for the full 18 months

Because you're learning, we take the maintenance worry off your plate entirely. Here's what's covered while you rent to own.

Repairs at no extra cost

If a chip or hashboard fails during the 18-month term, our on-site US techs fix it — included, with original parts and a fresh 90-day warranty on every repair.

Up to 3 months downtime, accounted for

Across the full term there can be up to three months of cumulative repair downtime. We're upfront about it: your rent stays due regardless of mining results, so budget the payment independently.

Your keys, not ours

Daily payouts land in a WowSuch MPC self-custody wallet. We never hold your coins — withdraw to any wallet, swap on DeFi, or cash out to USD after KYC.

One US facility, on-site team

Purpose-tuned for the L9, 24/7 monitored, repair techs on the same floor your chips run on. Inspect by video any time; visit in person at 50+ machines.

Never mine at a loss

If mining revenue ever drops below the $0.079 hosting cost, we curtail or self-mine until it's profitable again — so hosting never eats into what you've earned.

Paid every 24 hours

No monthly batches and no minimum balances held back. Your share of LTC + DOGE lands in your wallet every day, with fees already settled.
