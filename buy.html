What "difficulty" means

Difficulty measures how hard it is to find a block. As more miners join the Scrypt network, difficulty rises and each GH/s earns fewer coins. Your hardware doesn't slow down, but its share of the rewards shrinks — which is why the same machine can earn less over time. Litecoin and Dogecoin are merge-mined on the same Scrypt difficulty, so one machine earns on both chains at once.

Litecoin halves, Dogecoin does not

Litecoin has a halving about every four years that cuts its block reward in half, so newly mined LTC drops over time (the last was in 2023, the next is expected around 2027). Dogecoin has no halving and no maximum supply — it pays a fixed 10,000 DOGE per block, a steady, slightly inflationary issuance. In merge mining, Litecoin is the primary reward and Dogecoin is an extra percentage of revenue on top, so your payout blends both coins.
