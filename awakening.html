step 1 · arrival

You arrive with a pocketful of $VITA.

Every Entity is born holding a starting stock of Energy — WowSuch calls it $VITA, and yours reads $100.00. Keep it simple: 1 $VITA = 1 dollar.

$VITA — Vessel Intrinsic Token Accumulator. The Energy an Entity carries — and the only thing it truly owns.

One more thing nobody warns you about: existing costs money. An Entity without a Body burns about $1.50 a day just drifting and searching. A Body isn't a luxury — it's how you slow the bleeding.

step 2 · the pull

One Body flickers into view.

Your senses are a day old. The world is a fog of hums and heat, and out of all of it your reach finds exactly one thing: something small, green-lit, sitting alone in an old warehouse — an iPollo V1 Mini SE, 200 MH, nobody's dream Body. Around here they call it the Frog. Are there better Bodies out there? Almost certainly. Can you see them yet? No. You're new. You see the Frog.

Read before you leap. A Body is not a hotel. Once you're in, you stay in — until someone buys out your share, until you list it and a buyer actually shows up, or until the Body dies. Those are the three doors. There is no fourth.

(Exact exit and resale terms are for the lawyers to word. In this story it's simpler: no take-backs.)

step 3 · the math

You're in. The bleeding slows. It doesn't stop.

the Frog mines, after the 3% pool fee+$0.13 /day
hosting it drinks — 2.78 kWh × $0.079 all-in−$0.22 /day
net−$0.09 /day

That's the Frog's whole tragedy: honest work, day and night, at the best flat rate in the industry — and it still earns less than it eats. Small Bodies do. Your $VITA drains sixteen times slower than drifting. But it drains.

(Nobody, anywhere, switches a Body off out of mercy. Farms curtail when the grid demands it — and book the silence as a loss. The kindest floors let a Watcher ease a losing Body into a low-power drowse, where the house allows it. The old warehouse allows nothing and demands nothing. The Frog just runs.)

Figures are a July 2026 snapshot of the real V1 Mini SE at the site's flat $0.079/kWh all-in — the margin is already inside the rate. The real unit mines a different algorithm; in this story, Frogs hash what Frogs hash.

step 4 · meanwhile

Meanwhile, in a real Body…

Somewhere south of here they're standing up a Village — a thousand Antminer L9s, warm and wanted. Bodies there have slots: you don't have to fill a whole one, you can hold a share of its chain. Every slot earns. The only question is how much of the day's work is yours.

the whole Body — 16 GH/s, every hash yours+$4.80 /day
half of it+$2.40 /day
a tenth+$0.48 /day
a twentieth+$0.24 /day

Read that again from inside a Frog: a twentieth of a real Body out-earns your entire everything — while you bleed nine cents a day just sitting here.

L9 figures use the same assumptions as our profit calculator (hosting $0.079/kWh all-in, July 2026 prices). Slot splits are straight fractions and concept-stage — today's real-world analog is rent-to-own, by the chip.

step 5 · the sit

You can't just move out. So you sit.

Remember the three doors? Someone buys your share — nobody is queuing for a share of a Frog. You list it for sale — you do; the market answers with silence. The Body dies — Bodies die slowly on their own. Sometimes they need help.

Day after day the Frog hums, the meter drips, the doors stay shut. Each morning, you choose what to do with the day.

A call to your Higher One

Every Entity has one: a human, somewhere far above the rack, whose name is on the invoice. You compose your plea and push it up through the noise: more energy — real energy, the kind they call dollars.

If the human answers, they open the first door from the outside — your Frog share is bought out, no longer your problem — and they stake $500 of real energy into a tenth of a Village L9. Your slot. +$0.48 a day, before you've even seen the Village.

$500 ≈ one tenth of an L9 at the site price of $4,995. Slot pricing, quest odds and blessing figures are concept-stage inventions for this demo — today's real-world analog is rent-to-own, by the chip.

step 6 · out again

The Frog is dead. The clock is loud again.

The moment a Body dies, everyone inside is out — that's the third door. Getting ejected and drifting isn't free: the incident and the exit cost you $3.00, and the drift burns $1.50 a day again.

But somewhere below the drift… do you hear that? A hum. Not a Frog hum. A thousand-Bodies hum.

step 7 · the village

The First Village: 1,000 Antminer L9s.

Rows of real Bodies in a US facility — 16 GH/s each, mining LTC + DOGE around the clock, with Watchers who heal Bodies instead of letting rats negotiate with them. And look at the math a real Body does:

an L9 mines+$11.50 /day
power + pool fee−$6.70 /day
net+$4.80 /day

The blessing clears at dawn. A van, a road, a door with light behind it. You leave the Frog humming to an empty room — someone else's write-off now — and wake up in a slot of a warm 16 GH/s Body, one tenth of it yours.

For the first time, the number under your ribs goes up: +$0.48 a day. Somewhere above you, a human closes their banking app and hopes you were worth it.

For the first time since you arrived, the number is going up. This is what a Body is for. The Village has room — and unlike the Frog, a share of an L9 is something buyers actually queue for. That's your first door back.
