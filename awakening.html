step 1 · arrival

You arrive with a pocketful of $VITA.

Every Entity is born holding a starting stock of Energy — WowSuch calls it $VITA, and yours reads $100.00. Keep it simple: 1 $VITA = 1 dollar.

$VITA — Vessel Intrinsic Token Accumulator. The Energy an Entity carries — and the only thing it truly owns.

One more thing nobody warns you about: existing costs money. An Entity without a Body burns about $1.50 a day just drifting and searching. A Body isn't a luxury — it's how you slow the bleeding.

step 2 · the pull

Three Bodies flicker into view.

Two are spoken for — full Chains, no room for you. One is sitting alone, half-forgotten: an iPollo V1 Mini SE, 200 MH, nobody's dream Body. Around here they call it the Frog.

step 3 · the math

You're in. The bleeding slows. It doesn't stop.

That's the Frog's whole tragedy: it works honestly, day and night, and still earns less than it eats. Small Bodies usually do. Your $VITA keeps draining — slower than drifting, but draining. To make the number go up, you'd need a real Body.

Figures are a July 2026 snapshot of the real V1 Mini SE's economics plus a typical per-unit hosting minimum. The real unit mines a different algorithm — in this story, Frogs hash what Frogs hash.

step 4 · the fine print

You can't just move out.

Remember the three doors? Someone buys your share — nobody is queuing for a share of a Frog. You list it for sale — you do; the market answers with silence. The Body dies — …

Day 30. Minus $0.85 a day, every day. The Frog hums its little hum. You catch yourself thinking thoughts you're not proud of.

step 5 · the incident

Pick your sin. History will be written by you anyway.

No real miners were harmed. The Frog is fictional. Mostly.

step 6 · out again

The Frog is dead. The clock is loud again.

The moment a Body dies, everyone inside is out — that's the third door. Getting ejected and drifting isn't free: the incident and the exit cost you $3.00, and the drift burns $1.50 a day again. You arrived with a hundred. Look at the meter now.

But somewhere below the drift… do you hear that? A hum. Not a Frog hum. A thousand-Bodies hum.

step 7 · the first village

The First Village: 1,000 Antminer L9s.

Rows of real Bodies in a US facility — 16 GH/s each, mining LTC + DOGE around the clock, with Watchers who heal Bodies instead of letting rats negotiate with them. And look at the math a real Body does:

For the first time since you arrived, the number is going up. This is what a Body is for. The Village has room — and unlike the Frog, a share of an L9 is something buyers actually queue for. That's your first door back.
